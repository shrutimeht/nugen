P2P/NLD

Point-to-Point (PTP) is a direct connection between two devices, nodes, or endpoints. Data is transmitted in a dedicated circuit or channel, established before sending data. Examples include leased lines, VPNs, dial-up modems, and some wireless connections. PTP is used for dedicated internet access, WAN connections, private networks, and high-bandwidth applications, offering dedicated bandwidth, high security, low latency, and reliable connectivity, but can be more expensive and require moreÂ infrastructure.

National Long Distance (NLD) refers to telecommunications services that connect different geographic regions within a country. It involves transmitting voice, data, and multimedia content over long distances, typically through high-capacity fiber optic cables or microwave links. NLD networks are crucial for interconnecting cities, towns, and rural areas, facilitating seamless communication and data transfer across the nation. Telecom operators provide NLD services to businesses, government agencies, and individuals, enabling them to communicate efficiently over vast distances. NLD infrastructure plays a pivotal role in supporting economic activities, digital connectivity, and societal development by ensuring robust and reliable nationwide communication networks.
